About Aqua World

Dive Into the Ocean

Experience marine life like never before!

Aqua World is more than an aquarium; it's a vibrant celebration of marine biodiversity and an interactive journey through the wonders of the ocean. Whether you're a family seeking a fun day out, a student eager to learn, or a marine enthusiast fascinated by the ocean, Aqua World has something for everyone.

  • Home to over 10,000 aquatic species.
  • Interactive zones, including touch pools and feeding sessions.
  • Educational programs for all ages.
  • Conservation initiatives to protect marine ecosystems.

Ancient Beginnings

  • Mesopotamia (2500 BCE): The earliest known fish ponds were created in Mesopotamia, where fish were kept for food and religious purposes.
  • Egypt (1500 BCE): The ancient Egyptians kept fish in temple pools and private gardens. They saw fish as sacred symbols and decorative additions to their luxurious estates.
  • China (1000 BCE): The Chinese pioneered the art of ornamental fishkeeping, breeding goldfish in decorative ponds and jars.

The Renaissance Period

With the development of glassblowing techniques during the Renaissance, people began using glass containers to observe aquatic life indoors. This innovation laid the foundation for modern aquariums.

19th Century Innovations

  • 1840s: The invention of the first glass aquariums in England marked the beginning of modern aquarium culture. These were initially used to study aquatic life in controlled environments.
  • 1853: The London Zoo opened the first public aquarium, called the "Fish House," inspiring similar establishments worldwide.
  • 1880s: Advances in aeration and water circulation improved the viability of aquariums, allowing for a broader range of species to thrive.

20th Century Advancements

The 20th century saw rapid progress in aquarium technology, including filtration systems, artificial lighting, and temperature controls. These advancements made home aquariums more accessible and diverse,

Our Journey

  • 2010: Aqua World was founded with a mission to bring marine ecosystems to landlocked communities, offering educational and conservation-focused experiences.
  • 2012: Opened to the public with over 100 species on display, including rare and exotic marine life.
  • 2015: Introduced a groundbreaking coral restoration project in partnership with Ocean Conservation Trust.
  • 2020: Launched interactive virtual tours, reaching global audiences during the pandemic.

Today, Aqua World continues to innovate, combining education, conservation, and entertainment to inspire people to care for our oceans and the life within them.
